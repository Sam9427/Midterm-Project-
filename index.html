Colorism 101

Understanding Colorism

Colorism is discrimination based on skin tone, often favoring lighter skin over darker skin within communities of color.

Media representations often reinforce these biases, intentionally or otherwise. This website aims to educate and provide resources for understanding colorism and its effects in our society.

Colorism in the Media

One example of colorism in media can be seen in the casting of the film The Hate You Give. The novel features Starr Carter, described as a dark-skinned girl with coily hair, but in the movie, the role was given to a light-skinned actress with looser curls. This choice sparked backlash, as it highlights how dark-skinned actresses are often overlooked for “marketable” alternatives.
