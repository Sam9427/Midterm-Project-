Colorism In The Media

Like most of us, consuming media has quickly become a part of our everyday lives. Whether it's Instagram, TikTok, or Netflix, the options are endless, and unfortunately, colorism runs rampant throughout these spaces.

For instance, in the film adaptation of The Hate You Give, the main character Starr Carter is portrayed by a light-skinned actress, despite being described as a dark-skinned girl in the novel. This casting choice sparked criticism, as it exemplifies how dark-skinned characters are often overlooked in favor of "marketable" alternatives.

This practice is harmful because representation of dark-skinned Black women as main characters is rare. When such a character is altered to be lighter-skinned, it contributes to the erasure of Black stories and reinforces narrow beauty standards in media.
